About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Bioengineering at the UC Berkeley-UCSF Bioengineering Graduate Program. My research focuses on developing point-of-care diagnostics using CRISPR technology for rapid, multiplexed, and quantitative RNA detection.

I have developed Cas13-based diagnostic platforms for direct RNA detection, incorporating compact fluorescence detectors and multiplexing via barcoded beads. Additionally, I designed and deployed AI-powered mobile microscopy platforms for field diagnosis of soil-transmitted helminth eggs in stool samples in Bangladesh.

My passion lies in advancing digital healthcare technologies for improving public health, education, and accessibility. Beyond research, I am dedicated to education and mentorship, guiding students from underrepresented backgrounds in STEM fields.
